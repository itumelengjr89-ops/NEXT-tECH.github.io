Escalation Procedure

Follow these steps when the service desk agent cannot resolve an incident on-site.

Steps

  1. Attempt to resolve at the workstation using the Troubleshooting Checklist. Document every step in the Support Request Log.
  2. If unresolved after standard troubleshooting (15–30 minutes depending on urgency), gather full details and error messages.
  3. Determine whether the issue is a local workstation/configuration problem or requires escalation to Infrastructure/Applications.
  4. Escalate to the appropriate team with: call record reference, screenshots or error codes, steps attempted, and business impact (e.g., blocking revenue ops = High).
  5. Notify the Global Sales Consultant of escalation status and expected follow-up timeframe.

Escalation Matrix (example)

  • High / Very urgent — Escalate to IT Manager + Infrastructure on call
  • Average — Escalate to Tier 2 Application Support
  • Low — Create ticket and schedule for next business day

Always include the call reference, detailed description, steps taken, and business impact when escalating.
